Oto Ai

An AI enabled digital otoscope which classifies ear pathology at 90% accuracy. 2020 Aug - 2021 Apr | University of Pennsylvania

This device helps primary care physician identify pathology and capture images to send to a specialist. This project includes an easy to use button and encasing, a desktop app for Mac and PC which allows physicians to generate reports in a format that is compatible with EMRs and a transfer learning based CNN to classify the imagees in seconds. This project won the 2021 Senior Design Award for Technology and innovation.

Robotics

Programming a ROS Simulated Articulated Automated Robot

2020 Nov - 2021 Dec | University of Pennsylvania

This 3R atriculated robotic arm with a 2RP wrist-gripper was used ina robotics competition to stack blocks from a rotating field and static fields. The robots were programed from scratch in Matlab where path planning techniques such as A star and RRT where tested. Forward and inverse kinamtic algorithms where implemented from scratch and many frame changes were used to decide robot position. Decision making was key in maximizing the accuracy and the points gained. ROS and linux were used for testing on a virtual machine.

HealthSense

A company dedicated to IoT in Helathcare2017 Nov - 2019 Aug | Personal Project

With the grand vision of having all the devices in a hospital room monitored remotely and inferencing patients health, I designed a device that attaches to E cylinder oxygen tanks and tells you the pressure from a monitoring dashboard. I started this project after shadowing the respiratory therapists at Baptist Hospital in South Florida. I later piloted my device in Penn Medicine and Good Sheperd Penn Partners Rehab Center. The device and the dashboard saved dozens of hours of work every month which used to involve having to go room to room to visualize tanks. The device also saved hospitals money on refilling tanks. I put together a team formed a Deleware C Corperation and as CEO won 1st prize in the Rothberg Catalyzer and then entered Y Combinator in the summer of 2019. We raised 150,000 dollars at a 2.24 M dollar evaluation. After pivoting away from healthcare to keep up with the YC lifestyle this company is now Puzzl and I left to pursue my passion for healthcare.

Heterotopic Bone Segmentation

Creating a computer vision algorithm to seperate bone and soft tissue in micro CT images 2019 Apr - 2019 May| University of Pennsylvania

Fibrodysplasia ossificans progressiva or FOP is a rare disease where bone begins to form in soft tissue such as muscle, it is impoortant to study for the implications of growing bone in vitro and for strengthening bone those with more common diseases such as Osteoporosis. The study of fibrodysplasia ossificans progressiva by the Shore Labs at the University of Pennsylvania requires that CT images of mice be observed and consistant measurements of the ratio of soft tissue to bone be calcualted. Bofore my algorithm it would take hours for a research assistant or graduate student to look through the images and estimate where bone was to be found. With a large set of labeled data I created a training set and used pytorch to create a UNET that classified images. Additionally, more efficient image recognition and computer vision libraries were employed for use of the team.
